Chicken Pork Adobo

Description

Chicken pork adobo is a beloved Filipino dish that embodies the essence of comfort food. It features a richly flavored marinade of vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and spices, which tenderizes the meat while infusing it with a tangy and savory taste. Typically, a combination of chicken pieces and pork belly or shoulder is simmered slowly in this marinade until the meats are tender and infused with the robust flavors of the sauce. The dish is known for its versatility, as variations in ingredients and cooking techniques allow for regional and personal adaptations throughout the Philippines.

Each bite of chicken pork adobo offers a delightful contrast of textures, with the meat being both succulent and lightly caramelized from the reduction of the marinade. It's often served with steamed rice to soak up the flavorful sauce, which has a perfect balance of acidity and umami.

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken pieces (thighs, drumsticks, or a combination)
  • 500g pork belly or shoulder, cut into chunks
  • 1 head of garlic, minced
  • 1 cup vinegar (preferably cane vinegar or white vinegar)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon whole black peppercorns, crushed
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, to balance the acidity)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Cooking oil

Instructions

  1. Marinate the meats: In a bowl or a large resealable bag, combine the chicken and pork with soy sauce, minced garlic, bay leaf, whole black peppercorns, and vinegar. Mix well to coat the meat evenly. Let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour, or even overnight in the refrigerator for deeper flavor.
  2. Sauté the garlic: Heat a large pan or pot over medium heat. Add a tablespoon of cooking oil and sauté the minced garlic until fragrant and lightly golden. Be careful not to burn the garlic as it can turn bitter.
  3. Cook the meats: Add the marinated chicken and pork (reserve the marinade) to the pan with the sautéed garlic. Brown the meats on all sides for about 5-7 minutes.
  4. Simmer with marinade: Pour in the marinade mixture (soy sauce, vinegar, spices) into the pan. Add water enough to cover the meats. Bring to a boil then lower the heat to a simmer.
  5. Cook until tender:Cover the pan and let it simmer for about 30-40 minutes or until the meats are tender and cooked through. Stir occasionally to ensure even cooking and to prevent sticking.
  6. Adjust seasoning: Taste the adobo and adjust seasoning with salt and sugar if desired, to balance the flavors.
  7. Once the meats are tender and the sauce has reduced to your liking (it should be slightly thickened), remove from heat. Serve hot with steamed rice.

    8. This version of chicken pork adobo highlights the robust flavors of garlic, creating a deliciously savory dish that pairs perfectly with rice. Enjoy your meal!
